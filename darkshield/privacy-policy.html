DarkShield Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 25, 2026

Summary: DarkShield processes email data locally in your browser. No email content is stored permanently. External API lookups (VirusTotal, Google Safe Browsing) are optional and can be fully disabled.

1. What Data We Process

DarkShield analyzes the following data from emails displayed in your browser:

  • Email metadata: Sender address, display name, subject line
  • Links: URLs contained in email bodies (checked for phishing indicators)
  • Attachment metadata: File names, extensions, and sizes (files are not opened or downloaded)
  • Email headers: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication results

2. How Data Is Processed

All core threat analysis runs locally in your browser. This includes:

  • Phishing pattern detection
  • Homoglyph (lookalike domain) analysis
  • Social engineering and urgency language detection
  • Attachment risk scoring
  • Email authentication verification

3. External Services (Optional)

When enabled (Balanced or Performance privacy mode), DarkShield may send limited data to third-party services:

  • VirusTotal: URL hashes may be sent for reputation checks. Your API key is stored locally and never shared.
  • Google Safe Browsing: URL prefixes may be checked against Google's threat lists.

You can disable all external lookups by selecting Strict privacy mode in Settings. In Strict mode, no data leaves your browser.

4. Data Storage

  • Settings and preferences: Stored locally using Chrome's storage API
  • Scan statistics: Aggregate counts (emails scanned, threats detected) stored locally
  • Threat cache: Temporary cache of analysis results stored in memory; cleared when the browser closes
  • No email content is stored permanently

5. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Full email body content is not stored or transmitted
  • No personally identifiable information is collected
  • No telemetry or usage analytics are sent by default
  • No data is sold or shared with advertisers
  • No browsing history beyond email client pages is accessed

6. User Controls

You have full control over your data:

  • Privacy modes: Choose Strict (local only), Balanced, or Performance
  • Disable external lookups: Toggle off threat intelligence in Settings
  • Clear data: All locally stored data can be cleared from Settings
  • Uninstall: Removing the extension deletes all stored data

7. Permissions

DarkShield requests the following browser permissions:

  • activeTab: Access the current tab to analyze visible emails
  • storage: Save your settings and scan statistics locally
  • scripting: Inject the email analysis UI into email clients
  • webNavigation: Detect when you navigate to email pages
  • notifications: Alert you to high-risk emails
  • alarms: Schedule periodic threat intelligence updates
  • Host access to Gmail and Outlook: Required to analyze emails in those clients

8. Changes to This Policy

We will update this policy as needed. Material changes will be communicated through the extension's update notes.

9. Contact
